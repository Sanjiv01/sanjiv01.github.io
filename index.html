Classification of Tic-Tac-Toe Game Using Deep Neural Network

International Journal of Scientific Research in Science, Engineering and Technology · May 26, 2021

This study investigates the classic two-player game Tic-Tac-Toe, played on a 3x3 grid, where players aim to align three markers in a row to win. It presents the development of a machine learning classification model designed to predict the outcomes of Tic-Tac-Toe games between two players. Various machine learning methods are analyzed, including Decision Trees, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) with two neighbors, Support Vector Machines (SVM), Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) with one hidden layer, and Deep Neural Networks (DNN). The Tic-Tac-Toe dataset, sourced from the publicly available UCI repository, is trained and evaluated using these techniques.
